## What Is Ketamine and Why Is It Sought After?

Ketamine, often referred to as a dissociative anesthetic, has gained attention for its medical and recreational uses. Originally developed in the 1960s, it's used in veterinary and human medicine for anesthesia and pain management. In recent years, research has explored its potential in treating depression and PTSD, leading to FDA-approved forms like esketamine (Spravato) for clinical settings.

For those searching to buy ketamine, interest often stems from its unique effects: euphoria, altered perception, and short-term dissociation. However, sourcing it responsibly is crucial due to legal restrictions and health risks.

### Legal Status of Ketamine in 2026

As of 2026, ketamine remains a Schedule III controlled substance under the U.S. Controlled Substances Act, meaning it has accepted medical uses but high abuse potential. Similar classifications apply globally, with variations—e.g., it's Schedule 8 in Australia and Class B in the UK. Non-medical purchases are illegal in most places, with penalties including fines and imprisonment. Always verify current laws in your region.
